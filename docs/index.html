Who We Are

Zoloti Maky (Zuh-luh-tee Ma-key) is San Francisco's Ukrainian Dance Ensemble named after the California Golden Poppy (Zoloti meaning golden, and Maky, poppy). It pays tribute to the state they call home and honors the symbolic poppy of Ukrainian folklore.

Through dance, Zoloti Maky shares Ukrainian culture in a way that feels alive, joyful, and welcoming. Our performances range from high-energy group dances to softer, more intimate pieces, making them a great fit for festivals, weddings, and private events. We appear every year at Ukrainian Independence Day in Golden Gate Park, the Sebastopol Ukrainian-Japanese Festival, and Ukrainian Christmas celebrations, and have also performed at Toys for Tots, Dumplings Festivals, on a San Jose TV show, and at countless small private parties. We love connecting with audiences and bringing a warm Ukrainian spirit to every celebration we're part of.

Our shows celebrate traditional Ukrainian dances and the stories behind them. Audiences love the energetic Hopak, the warm and welcoming Pryvit, the vibrant Hutsulka, and the tender, heartfelt shawl dance to Shedryk (Carol of the Bells). Every performance is full of energy, color, and emotion, designed to make guests smile, cheer, and feel a true connection to Ukrainian culture.
